# Product

## Register

product

## Users

BrainBar is for people who keep meaningful work in local Markdown or Graphify-compatible vaults and need a fast way to re-enter, inspect, and navigate that knowledge graph on macOS.

Primary users are builders, researchers, writers, and agent-assisted operators who already have graph data and want the graph to become an operating surface, not a decorative visualization.

## Product Purpose

BrainBar turns local Graphify output into a native macOS control center for exploring notes, recent changes, graph relationships, and local agent activity.

Success means a user can open the app, understand where they are in the graph, jump to a relevant note, inspect surrounding context, trace paths between ideas, and trust that the app remains local-first and non-destructive.

## Brand Personality

Calm, spatial, precise.

BrainBar should feel like a premium local graph instrument: restrained, native, focused, and technically capable without looking like a debugging panel or a generic SaaS dashboard.

## Anti-references

- Cheap Electron-style dark dashboards.
- Glossy pill-heavy controls that feel plastic or dated.
- Nested cards and stacked panels that make the graph feel boxed in.
- Noisy diagnostic sidebars where every feature competes at the same visual weight.
- Generic AI assistant UI that implies remote intelligence instead of local graph control.
- Decorative animation or color that does not clarify graph state.

## Design Principles

- Graph first: chrome and panels should recede so the graph remains the primary surface.
- Native restraint: prefer macOS-like hierarchy, spacing, and state treatment over custom decoration.
- Local-first trust: UI copy and states should make local, runtime-only behavior clear without over-explaining.
- Dense but legible: BrainBar can show complex graph data, but sidebar sections must have clear hierarchy, calm spacing, and scannable actions.
- State before spectacle: motion, color, glow, and emphasis should communicate selection, activity, or navigation state.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

BrainBar should target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls, preserve clear focus and hover states, avoid relying on color alone for critical state, and respect reduced-motion settings.

The product is dark-mode first, but muted text, borders, and disabled states must remain readable on deep surfaces.
